\title{The Arrow of Time from Petz Recovery}

\author{Sheng-Kai Huang}
\email{akai@fawstudio.com}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We define a temporal asymmetry parameter
$\tauasym = 1 - F$, where $F$ is the fidelity of Petz recovery,
and argue that it provides a continuous, operational measure of
the arrow of time: $\tauasym = 0$ for closed, unitary evolution
and $\tauasym > 0$ for irreversible processes, bounded by entropy
production via $\tauasym \leq 1 - \exp(-\Sigma/2)$ (restating
the universal recovery bound of Junge et~al.).
Because the Petz map is the unique retrodiction functor satisfying
Bayesian consistency, this single parameter links the quantum
eraser (where $\tauasym = 0$ because the signal-idler pair is
closed), quantum error correction (where $\tauasym > 0$ sets the
decoder's task), and thermodynamic irreversibility (where
$\tauasym$ is bounded by dissipation).
We establish two new results:
the recovery bound $F^2 \geq \exp(-\Delta D)$ is exactly
saturated if and only if the channel acts as a quantum sufficient
statistic, and
$\sqrt{\tauasym}$ satisfies a triangle inequality
under channel composition---a property unique to the standard
Petz map.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Petz recovery map~\cite{Petz1986,Petz1988} is a near-optimal
quantum error correction (QEC) decoder~\cite{Barnum2002}, the
\emph{unique} retrodiction functor satisfying Bayesian
consistency~\cite{Parzygnat2023}, and the reverse channel in
quantum Crooks theorems~\cite{Kwon2019}.  It also appears in
entanglement wedge reconstruction~\cite{Chen2020} and as a
physical reversal via dilations~\cite{Aw2024}.
Concurrent work by Bai, Buscemi, and
Scarani~\cite{Buscemi2024} develops stochastic entropy
production using the same Petz-retrodiction foundation.

In this Letter, we offer a physical interpretation of this
mathematical unity.  We define a temporal asymmetry parameter
$\tauasym = 1 - F$, where $F$ is the Petz recovery fidelity, and
argue that $\tauasym$ provides a continuous, operational measure of
the arrow of time.

The interpretive framework rests on known results assembled into a
single picture.
The quantum eraser~\cite{Scully1982,Kim2000} is a physical
instantiation of Petz recovery, and
the uniqueness of the Petz map as retrodiction functor (due to
Parzygnat--Buscemi~\cite{Parzygnat2023}) explains why it also
serves as a near-optimal QEC
decoder~\cite{Barnum2002,Junge2018}.
The universal recovery bound of Junge
et~al.~\cite{Junge2018} (Corollary~5.1 therein) and the
Fawzi--Renner inequality~\cite{Fawzi2015}, combined with the
Kolchinsky--Wolpert decomposition~\cite{Kolchinsky2017} and the
Reeb--Wolf bound~\cite{ReebWolf2014}, yield a chain linking
recovery fidelity to entropy production.
The logical flow is:
\begin{equation}
\text{Eraser} \xrightarrow{\text{Petz}} \text{Retrodiction}
\xrightarrow{} \text{QEC}
\xrightarrow{} \text{Thermo.}
\label{eq:flow}
\end{equation}

The physical content---that Petz recovery failure quantifies the
arrow of time---is made precise in Sec.~\ref{sec:thermo}.

We present two new mathematical results.  First, we characterize
exactly when the Junge et~al.\ bound
$F^2 \geq \exp(-\Delta D)$ is saturated: if and only if
the channel acts as a quantum sufficient statistic
(Theorem~\ref{thm:saturation}).
Second, we prove that $\sqrt{\tauasym}$ satisfies a triangle
inequality under channel composition---a structural property
unique to the Petz map that fails for the rotated Petz map
(Theorem~\ref{thm:composition}).

\section{The Petz Recovery Map}
\label{sec:petz}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Given a quantum channel $\chan: \mathcal{B}(\hilb_A) \to
\mathcal{B}(\hilb_B)$ and a faithful reference state $\sigma \in
\mathcal{S}(\hilb_A)$, the \emph{Petz recovery map} is
\begin{equation}
\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}(\rho) = \sigma^{1/2}\,
\chan^\dagger\!\big(\chan(\sigma)^{-1/2}\,\rho\,
\chan(\sigma)^{-1/2}\big)\,\sigma^{1/2},
\label{eq:petz}
\end{equation}
where $\chan^\dagger$ is the Hilbert--Schmidt adjoint of $\chan$.
The map $\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}$ is completely positive and trace-preserving
(CPTP)~\cite{Petz1986}, and satisfies exact recovery
$\Petz_{\sigma,\chan} \circ \chan = \id$ on the support of $\sigma$ if
and only if the data-processing inequality (DPI) for relative entropy is
saturated: $D(\sigma\|\tau) = D(\chan(\sigma)\|\chan(\tau))$ for all
$\tau$~\cite{Petz1988}.

Table~\ref{tab:dictionary} shows how the mathematical ingredients of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:petz} map to physical meaning in each domain.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\caption{\label{tab:dictionary}Physical interpretation of the Petz map
$\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}$ across four domains.}
\begin{ruledtabular}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
& \textbf{Eraser} & \textbf{Time Rev.} & \textbf{QEC} & \textbf{Thermo.} \\
\colrule
$\chan$ & Path mark & Fwd.\ evol. & Noise & Irrev.\ proc. \\
$\sigma$ & Entangled & Prior & Code state & Gibbs \\
$\Petz$ & Erasure & Retrodiction & Decoder & Rev.\ proc. \\
$\Delta D$ & $0$ (closed) & Info.\ loss & Code dist. & $\beta W_{\text{diss}}$ \\
\end{tabular}
\end{ruledtabular}
\end{table}

\section{Uniqueness of Retrodiction}
\label{sec:retrodiction}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

We recall the uniqueness result of Parzygnat and
Buscemi~\cite{Parzygnat2023}, which is central to our
interpretation.

\begin{theorem}[Retrodiction Uniqueness
  (Parzygnat--Buscemi~\cite{Parzygnat2023})]
\label{thm:unique}
Let $\mathsf{C}$ be the category of finite-dimensional $C^*$-algebras
with state-preserving CP maps as morphisms. A \emph{retrodiction functor}
is a contravariant functor $R: \mathsf{C} \to \mathsf{C}^{\mathrm{op}}$
satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\emph{(i)}] \emph{Preservation:} $R$ sends $(\chan,\sigma)$ to a
  CPTP map.
\item[\emph{(ii)}] \emph{Bayesian consistency:} For composable channels
  $\chan_1, \chan_2$ and prior $\sigma$,
  \begin{equation}
  R(\chan_2 \circ \chan_1,\,\sigma)
    = R(\chan_1,\,\sigma) \circ R(\chan_2,\,\chan_1(\sigma)).
  \label{eq:functor}
  \end{equation}
\item[\emph{(iii)}] \emph{Normalization:} $R(\id,\sigma) = \id$.
\item[\emph{(iv)}] \emph{Classical limit:} On commutative subalgebras,
  $R$ reduces to classical Bayes' rule.
\end{enumerate}
Then, for faithful $\sigma$,
$R(\chan,\sigma) = \Petz_{\sigma,\chan}$ is the unique such functor.
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof sketch.}---Axioms (ii) and (iv) force the
$\sigma^{1/2}(\cdot)\sigma^{1/2}$ sandwich structure. For commutative
algebras, $R$ must be the Bayesian inverse
$R(N,p)(q)_i = p_i N_{j|i}/(\sum_k p_k N_{j|k})$, which in matrix
form is $p^{1/2} N^T (Np)^{-1/2} (\cdot) (Np)^{-1/2} N p^{1/2}$.
By the functoriality axiom~\eqref{eq:functor} and the Stinespring
dilation theorem, this extends uniquely to the noncommutative
setting as Eq.~\eqref{eq:petz}. The full proof is in
Ref.~\cite{Parzygnat2023}; see also~\cite{Fullwood2023} for the
Bayesian framework and Supplemental Material~\cite{SM} for a
self-contained derivation. \hfill$\square$

\emph{Quantum eraser as Petz recovery.}---In the quantum eraser, a
signal-idler pair is prepared in the entangled state
$\ket{\psi}_{SE} = (\ket{A,d_A} + \ket{B,d_B})/\sqrt{2}$, where
$\ket{A},\ket{B}$ are path states and $\ket{d_A},\ket{d_B}$ are
orthogonal detector states. The which-path channel is
$\chan = \Tr_E$, giving the fully decohered signal state
$\rho_S = \frac{1}{2}(\op{A}{A} + \op{B}{B})$.
To our knowledge, the following explicit identification has not
appeared previously (concurrent work by Bai, Buscemi, and
Scarani~\cite{Buscemi2024} develops related ideas in the stochastic
entropy production setting).
Applying the Petz recovery~\eqref{eq:petz} with
$\sigma = \op{\psi}{\psi}_{SE}$ and $\chan = \Tr_E$:
\begin{equation}
\Petz_{\sigma,\Tr_E}(\rho_S) =
\op{\psi}{\psi}_{SE},
\label{eq:eraser_recovery}
\end{equation}
which is exact because $\Tr_E$ saturates the DPI for pure bipartite
states~\cite{SM}. The ``erasure measurement'' on the idler (projecting
onto $(\ket{d_A} \pm \ket{d_B})/\sqrt{2}$) is the physical
implementation of this Petz recovery.
Strictly, the idler measurement is a \emph{quantum instrument}
(a collection of CP maps summing to a CPTP map); conditioned on
outcome~$\pm$, the effective map on the signal is CP but not
trace-preserving. The Petz map $\Petz_{\sigma,\Tr_E}$ itself is
CPTP and recovers the full entangled state~\eqref{eq:eraser_recovery};
the subsequent projection onto $\ket{\pm}$ selects the subensemble
where the coherent superposition $\ket{A} \pm \ket{B}$ is restored.
The functoriality axiom~\eqref{eq:functor}
corresponds to the sequential structure of the
eraser: composing the which-path marking with the idler measurement
decomposes as retrodiction from the idler, then from the path.

\section{From Retrodiction to Error Correction}
\label{sec:qec}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The uniqueness of the Petz map as retrodiction functor connects
naturally to its role as a QEC decoder.  The following collects
known results; our contribution is the interpretive link.

\begin{theorem}[Near-Optimal QEC via Petz Recovery
  (Barnum--Knill~\cite{Barnum2002},
   Junge~et~al.~\cite{Junge2018})]
\label{thm:qec}
Let $\chan$ be a noise channel acting on a $[\![n,k,d]\!]$ stabilizer
code $\code$ with code projector $P$ and maximally mixed code state
$\sigma_{\code} = P/\dim\code$. Let
$\epsilon_{\mathrm{KL}} = \max_{a,b}\norm{P E_a^\dagger E_b P
- c_{ab} P}$ measure deviation from the Knill--Laflamme
conditions~\cite{KnillLaflamme1997}, where $\{E_a\}$ are Kraus operators
of $\chan$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\emph{(a)}] If $\epsilon_{\mathrm{KL}} = 0$ (exact QEC),
  $\Petz_{\sigma_{\code},\chan} \circ \chan = \id$ on $\code$.
\item[\emph{(b)}] In general, the universal recovery theorem
  of Junge et~al.~\cite{Junge2018} (Corollary~5.1 therein)
  guarantees the existence of a \emph{rotated} Petz map
  $\tilde{\Petz}_{\sigma,\chan}$ such that:
  \begin{equation}
  F\big(\rho,\,\tilde{\Petz}_{\sigma_{\code},\chan}(\chan(\rho))\big)^2
  \geq \exp\!\big(-\Delta D\big),
  \label{eq:recovery_bound}
  \end{equation}
  where $\Delta D = D(\rho\|\sigma_\code) -
  D(\chan(\rho)\|\chan(\sigma_\code))$ is the relative entropy
  decrease.  The standard Petz map satisfies the weaker
  Barnum--Knill bound~\cite{Barnum2002}:
  $F_e \geq \frac{1}{2}F_{e,\mathrm{opt}}^2$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof.}---(a) When the Knill--Laflamme conditions hold exactly,
the DPI is saturated for all code states: $D(\rho\|\sigma_\code) =
D(\chan(\rho)\|\chan(\sigma_\code))$ for all $\rho$ supported on
$\code$. By Petz's sufficiency theorem~\cite{Petz1988}, the Petz
recovery is exact. (b)~The rotated Petz map is
$\tilde{\Petz}(\cdot) = \int\!dt\;p(t)\,\sigma^{it}
\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}(\chan(\sigma)^{-it}(\cdot)
\chan(\sigma)^{it})\sigma^{-it}$,
where $p(t) = (\pi/2)(\cosh(\pi t)+1)^{-1}$; the bound follows
from Ref.~\cite{Junge2018}. \hfill$\square$

QEC decoding is therefore retrodiction: the decoder
retrodicts the original $\rho$ from the corrupted
$\chan(\rho)$ using the code state as prior.  The Petz map is the
unique Bayesian-consistent choice
(Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}), and its
near-optimality~\cite{Barnum2002} can be understood as a
consequence of this uniqueness.

\section{The Thermodynamic Structure}
\label{sec:thermo}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

When the reference state $\sigma$ is a fixed point of the channel
(e.g., a Gibbs state at thermal equilibrium), known results combine
into a chain linking recovery fidelity to entropy production.  We
state this chain to establish the connection to the temporal
asymmetry parameter $\tauasym$.

\begin{proposition}[Master Inequality Chain
  (assembling~\cite{Junge2018,Fawzi2015,Kolchinsky2017,ReebWolf2014})]
\label{thm:master}
For any quantum channel $\chan$, reference state $\sigma$, and state
$\rho$:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
-\!\log F^2 \;\leq\; I(A;E|B)_\omega
\;\leq\; \Sigma
\;\leq\; \Delta D
}
\label{eq:master}
\end{equation}
where $F = F(\rho,\,\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}(\chan(\rho)))$ is the recovery
fidelity; $I(A;E|B)_\omega$ is the conditional mutual information of
the Stinespring complementary output; $\Sigma$ is the entropy production\footnote{For
ideal (infinite, memoryless) thermal baths, the total entropy production
of the Kolchinsky--Wolpert decomposition~\cite{Kolchinsky2017} and the
accessible entropy production of Reeb--Wolf~\cite{ReebWolf2014} coincide
with $\Delta D$.  The ordering $I(A;E|B) \leq \Sigma \leq \Delta D$
requires a Markovian bath assumption and is non-trivial only for
finite-size baths; see the Supplemental Material~\cite{SM} for
discussion.  The unconditional bound $-\!\log F^2 \leq \Delta D$
holds without this assumption.}; and
$\Delta D = D(\rho\|\sigma) - D(\chan(\rho)\|\chan(\sigma))$ is the
relative entropy decrease. When $\sigma = e^{-\beta H}/Z$ is a Gibbs
state:
\begin{equation}
\Delta D = \beta\, W_{\mathrm{diss}} = \Delta S_{\mathrm{irr}}/\kB,
\label{eq:gibbs}
\end{equation}
yielding a thermodynamic bound on recovery:
$F^2 \geq e^{-\Delta S_{\mathrm{irr}}/\kB}$.
\end{proposition}

\emph{Proof.}---Here $F$ denotes the fidelity with the rotated
Petz map~$\tilde{\Petz}_{\sigma,\chan}$ of
Ref.~\cite{Junge2018}.

\emph{Step 1} (Fidelity bound): By the universal recovery
theorem~\cite{Junge2018},
$F^2 \geq \exp(-\Delta D)$, so $-\log F^2 \leq \Delta D$.

\emph{Step 2} (CMI bound): By Fawzi--Renner~\cite{Fawzi2015},
$I(A;E|B) \geq -\log F^2$ via the strengthened data processing
inequality applied to the Stinespring dilation, giving the leftmost
bound.

\emph{Step 3} (Entropy production): For a thermodynamic process
with Gibbs reference state $\sigma = e^{-\beta H}/Z$, the entropy
production satisfies $\Sigma \geq I(A;E|B)$ because the Landauer
component $\Sigma_{\mathrm{Landauer}} = D(\rho\|\sigma) -
D(\chan(\rho)\|\chan(\sigma))$ equals $\Delta D \geq I(A;E|B)$
(by Steps 1--2), and $\Sigma \geq \Sigma_{\mathrm{Landauer}}$
by the Kolchinsky--Wolpert
decomposition~\cite{Kolchinsky2017}.

\emph{Step 4} (Second law): The relative entropy decrease
$\Delta D \geq \Sigma$ by Reeb--Wolf~\cite{ReebWolf2014}:
the free-energy dissipation exceeds the entropy production by
system-bath correlations and bath displacement.

The tightness conditions are physically transparent:
$F^2 = 1$ iff exact QEC (Knill--Laflamme satisfied);
$I(A;E|B) = 0$ iff Markov condition (no information leaks to
environment); $\Sigma = 0$ iff thermodynamically reversible process.
See Supplemental Material~\cite{SM} for the detailed derivation
including the Gibbs specialization~\eqref{eq:gibbs}. \hfill$\square$

\emph{Quantum Crooks connection.}---When $\sigma = \sigma_\beta =
e^{-\beta H}/Z$, the Petz map becomes $\Petz_{\sigma_\beta,\chan}$
with the structural factor $\sigma_\beta^{1/2} = e^{-\beta H/2}/\sqrt{Z}$
---exactly the ``half Boltzmann weight'' appearing in the KMS condition
of modular theory. Kwon and Kim~\cite{Kwon2019} proved that this
$\Petz_{\sigma_\beta,\chan}$ is precisely the reverse channel in the
quantum Crooks fluctuation theorem:
\begin{equation}
\frac{p_F(w)}{p_R(-w)} = e^{\beta(w - \Delta F)},
\label{eq:crooks}
\end{equation}
where $p_F(w)$ and $p_R(-w)$ are forward and Petz-reversed work
distributions. The detailed balance condition
$\Petz_{\sigma,\chan} = \chan$ characterizes thermodynamically
reversible processes---those that are their own Petz reversal.

\emph{Temporal asymmetry parameter.}---The infidelity
$1 - F$ of the Petz recovery is a standard measure of how far a
channel deviates from reversibility. We give it a physical name:
\begin{equation}
\tauasym \;=\; 1 - F\!\big(\rho,\,
  \tilde{\Petz}_{\sigma,\chan}(\chan(\rho))\big).
\label{eq:tau}
\end{equation}
This parameter quantifies the degree to which the channel $\chan$
distinguishes past from future when $\sigma$ is the reference state.
Two Petz maps appear in this work. The retrodiction
uniqueness theorem~\cite{Parzygnat2023} singles out
the \emph{standard} Petz map $\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}$ as the unique
retrodiction functor; the quantum Crooks theorem likewise uses
$\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}$ as the physical reverse channel.
The bound~\eqref{eq:recovery_bound} and the definition of $\tauasym$
require the \emph{rotated} map $\tilde{\Petz}$, which
averages $\Petz$ over modular automorphisms
$\sigma^{it}(\cdot)\sigma^{-it}$.
For a thermal reference $\sigma = e^{-\beta H}/Z$, the rotation
$\sigma^{it} = e^{-it\beta H}/Z^{it}$ is simply unitary time
evolution generated by $H$, so $\tilde{\Petz}$ smooths
the standard Petz map over the system's own Hamiltonian dynamics.
This thermal averaging handles quantum noncommutativity
$[\rho,\sigma] \neq 0$ and ensures that the fidelity bound
$F^2 \geq \exp(-\Delta D)$ holds universally.
When $[\rho,\sigma] = 0$ (classical limit) or $\Delta D = 0$
(exact recovery), $\tilde{\Petz}$ reduces to $\Petz$ and the
distinction vanishes.
For a unitary channel $\chan = \mathcal{U}$, the Petz map reduces
to $\mathcal{U}^\dagger$ and $F = 1$, so $\tauasym = 0$: closed
systems have no temporal asymmetry.  More generally,
$F^2 \geq \exp(-\Sigma)$ gives
\begin{equation}
\tauasym \;\leq\; 1 - \exp(-\Sigma/2),
\label{eq:tau_bound}
\end{equation}
which enforces $\tauasym = 0$ whenever $\Sigma = 0$.
The converse also holds.  For faithful $\sigma$
(the Crooks link requires Gibbs $\sigma$;
see~\cite{SM}, Sec.~S11):
\begin{equation}
\tauasym = 0 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \Sigma = 0
\;\Longleftrightarrow\; I(A;E|B) = 0
\;\Longleftrightarrow\; \text{QMC},
\label{eq:equiv_chain}
\end{equation}
where QMC denotes the quantum Markov chain
condition~\cite{Fawzi2015}. Each individual equivalence is
established~\cite{Petz1988,Fawzi2015,Landi2021}; the full
chain~\eqref{eq:equiv_chain} states them as a single result.
The arrow of time ($\tauasym > 0$) is equivalent to environmental
coupling ($I(A;E|B) > 0$), entropy production ($\Sigma > 0$),
and imperfect recoverability ($F < 1$).

The quantum eraser illustrates this directly.
The signal-idler pair is a closed bipartite system with
$\chan = \Tr_E$ and $\sigma = \ket{\psi}\bra{\psi}_{SE}$.
Since $I(A;E|B) = 0$ and $\Sigma = 0$, the equivalence chain
gives $\tauasym = 0$: the Petz map achieves exact recovery
(Sec.~\ref{sec:retrodiction}).
By contrapositive, recovery of interference
implies prior superposition~\cite{SM}.
In Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment~\cite{Wheeler1978},
$\tauasym = 0$ implies the temporal order of measurements is
irrelevant~\cite{Zurek2003}: the ``need to wait'' for the idler
measurement arises only when one traces out the idler
($\tauasym > 0$).

\section{New Result I: Bound Saturation Theorem}
\label{sec:saturation}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

We characterize exactly when the Junge et~al.\ bound
$F^2 \geq \exp(-\Delta D)$ is saturated.  The answer connects
Petz recovery to the classical theory of sufficient statistics.

\begin{theorem}[Saturation of the Recovery Bound]
\label{thm:saturation}
Let\/ $\chan$ be a CPTP map with $\sigma = I/d$ and
$\rho = \ket{\psi}\!\bra{\psi}$ a pure state.  Write
$\omega = \chan(\rho)$ and $\tau = \chan(\sigma)$.  Then
\begin{equation}
F^2\!\big(\rho,\,\Petz_{\sigma,\chan}(\chan(\rho))\big)
= \exp(-\Delta D)
\label{eq:saturation}
\end{equation}
if and only if\footnote{All directions are proved analytically.
The necessity of condition~(i) ($[\omega,\tau]\neq 0$ precludes
saturation) follows from the classical inequality
$\sqrt{ab} < L(a,b)$ between the geometric and logarithmic means;
for $d = 2$ the proof is non-perturbative
(see~\cite{SM}, Sec.~S17).}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\emph{(i)}] $[\omega, \tau] = 0$
  \emph{(channel outputs commute);}
\item[\emph{(ii)}] $\omega_i/\tau_i = c$ for all $i$ with
  $\omega_i > 0$
  \emph{(constant likelihood ratio on support).}
\end{enumerate}
When these hold, $F^2 = c/d$ and $\Delta D = \ln(d/c)$,
where $c = \omega_i/\tau_i$ is the common ratio.
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof.}---\emph{Sufficiency:}
When (i)--(ii) hold, $\tau^{-1/2}\omega\,\tau^{-1/2}
= c\,P_{\mathrm{supp}(\omega)}$.
Since each Kraus vector $E_k\ket{\psi}$ lies in the range of
$\omega$, we have $P_{\mathrm{supp}(\omega)}E_k\ket{\psi}
= E_k\ket{\psi}$ for all $k$.  Hence
$F^2 = \tfrac{1}{d}\,\bra{\psi}\chan^\dagger(c\,
P_{\mathrm{supp}(\omega)})\ket{\psi}
= \tfrac{c}{d}\sum_k\!\norm{E_k\ket{\psi}}^2 = c/d$.
Meanwhile $D(\omega\|\tau) = \sum_i \omega_i\ln(c) = \ln c$,
so $\exp(-\Delta D) = \exp(\ln c - \ln d) = c/d$.

\emph{Necessity:}
When (i) holds, in the joint eigenbasis of $\omega$ and $\tau$,
$F^2 = \tfrac{1}{d}\sum_i \omega_i^2/\tau_i$
(weighted arithmetic mean of $x_i = \omega_i/\tau_i$)
and $\exp(-\Delta D) = \tfrac{1}{d}\prod_i x_i^{\omega_i}$
(weighted geometric mean).  By the weighted AM--GM inequality,
$F^2 \geq \exp(-\Delta D)$ with equality if and only if all
$x_i$ are equal on the support---i.e., condition~(ii).
When (i) fails ($[\omega,\tau]\neq 0$), perturbation in the
$\tau$-eigenbasis shows that each off-diagonal mode $(j,k)$
contributes a positive gap
$\propto |\omega_{jk}|^2 [1/\sqrt{t_j t_k} - 1/L(t_j,t_k)]$,
where $L(a,b) = (a-b)/\ln(a/b)$ is the logarithmic mean.
The classical inequality $\sqrt{ab} < L(a,b)$ for $a \neq b$
ensures strict inequality whenever $[\omega,\tau] \neq 0$
(see~\cite{SM}, Sec.~S17).
\hfill$\square$

\emph{Sufficient-statistic interpretation.}---In classical
statistics, a statistic $T$ is \emph{sufficient} for
discriminating $P$ from $Q$ iff the likelihood ratio $P/Q$ is
constant on each level set of $T$.  Conditions~(i)--(ii) are
the quantum analogue: the channel $\chan$ acts as a quantum
sufficient statistic for the pair $\{\rho, \sigma\}$,
preserving the distinguishability ratio uniformly on the
support. Saturation of the Petz bound is equivalent to
sufficiency of the channel.

\emph{Examples.}---(a)~Amplitude damping
$\chan_\gamma^{\mathrm{AD}}$, $\rho = \ket{0}\!\bra{0}$:
$\omega = \ket{0}\!\bra{0}$ (pure), $\tau = ((1\!+\!\gamma)\ket{0}\!\bra{0}
+ (1\!-\!\gamma)\ket{1}\!\bra{1})/2$, ratio $c = 2/(1+\gamma)$,
$F^2 = 1/(1+\gamma)$. Saturated.
(b)~Completely depolarizing $\chan(\rho) = I/d$:
$\omega = \tau = I/d$, ratio $c = 1$, $F^2 = 1/d$. Saturated
for \emph{all} pure inputs.
(c)~Amplitude damping, $\rho = \ket{1}\!\bra{1}$:
ratios $\omega_0/\tau_0 \neq \omega_1/\tau_1$. Not saturated.
(d)~Depolarizing $0 < p < 1$: $\omega \neq \tau$, ratios
unequal. Not saturated.

\emph{Experimental relevance.}---Amplitude damping ($T_1$ decay)
is the dominant noise in superconducting qubits and ion traps,
and Petz recovery has been implemented on both
platforms~\cite{Png2025,Singh2025}.  The prediction
$F^2 = 1/(1+\gamma)$ for ground-state input is directly
testable.

\section{New Result II: Compositional Sub-additivity of $\tauasym$}
\label{sec:composition}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Petz map's functoriality~\eqref{eq:functor} implies a
geometric structure on $\tauasym$ that other recovery maps lack.

\begin{theorem}[Composition Sub-additivity]
\label{thm:composition}
For sequential channels $\chan_1, \chan_2$ and reference state
$\sigma$, the Petz recovery $\tauasym$ satisfies:
\begin{equation}
\sqrt{\tauasym_{12}} \;\leq\; \sqrt{\tauasym_1}
  + \sqrt{\tauasym_2^{\mathrm{eff}}},
\label{eq:composition}
\end{equation}
where $\tauasym_{12} = 1 - F(\rho,\,\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_2\circ\chan_1}
\circ (\chan_2\!\circ\!\chan_1)(\rho))$,\;
$\tauasym_1 = 1 - F(\rho,\,\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_1}
\circ \chan_1(\rho))$,\;
and $\tauasym_2^{\mathrm{eff}} =
1 - F(\chan_1(\rho),\,
\Petz_{\chan_1(\sigma),\chan_2}
\circ \chan_2(\chan_1(\rho)))$
is the second-stage cost evaluated at the
\emph{evolved} state $\chan_1(\rho)$ with \emph{updated}
reference $\chan_1(\sigma)$.
\end{theorem}

\emph{Proof.}---Three ingredients.
(1)~\emph{Functoriality}~\eqref{eq:functor}:
$\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_2\circ\chan_1} =
\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_1} \circ
\Petz_{\chan_1(\sigma),\chan_2}$.
(2)~\emph{Bures triangle inequality}: for any three states
$\rho, \rho_1, \rho_{12}$,
$\sqrt{1-F(\rho,\rho_{12})} \leq
\sqrt{1-F(\rho,\rho_1)} +
\sqrt{1-F(\rho_1,\rho_{12})}$.
(3)~\emph{Data-processing inequality}:
$\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_1}$ is CPTP, so
$F(\rho_1, \rho_{12}) =
F(\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_1}\circ\Petz_{\chan_1(\sigma),\chan_2}
\circ\chan_2\circ\chan_1(\rho),\,
\Petz_{\sigma,\chan_1}\circ\chan_1(\rho))
\geq F(\Petz_{\chan_1(\sigma),\chan_2}
\circ\chan_2(\chan_1(\rho)),\, \chan_1(\rho))$.
Combining gives~\eqref{eq:composition}.
See~\cite{SM}, Sec.~S18.\hfill$\square$

\emph{Remark.}---The reference for the second stage
\emph{must} be the Bayesian-updated $\chan_1(\sigma)$, not the
original~$\sigma$.  With the original reference, the inequality
fails numerically.  This is physically meaningful:
irreversibility decomposes additively across sequential
processes only when each stage's cost is measured using the
updated prior---the hallmark of Bayesian inference.

\emph{Uniqueness to Petz.}---The rotated Petz map
$\tilde{\Petz}$ of Ref.~\cite{Junge2018}, which gives tighter
fidelity bounds, does \emph{not} satisfy
Eq.~\eqref{eq:composition}: numerical tests find violations
for non-unital channel pairs (see~\cite{SM}, Sec.~S18).
This provides a new structural distinction between the two
recovery maps beyond what the categorical axioms~\cite{Parzygnat2023}
alone imply.  Note that
Theorem~\ref{thm:composition} uses the \emph{standard} Petz map,
while the bound~\eqref{eq:tau_bound} uses the \emph{rotated} map.
For commuting $[\rho,\sigma] = 0$ the two maps coincide; in general
the rotated map achieves higher fidelity, so
$\tauasym_{\mathrm{std}} \geq \tauasym_{\mathrm{rot}}$.

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Observations from the $\tauasym$ Framework}
\label{sec:observations}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Two further observations follow from interpreting existing results
through $\tauasym$.

\emph{Observation~1: Post-selection as thermodynamic
filtering.}---For a stabilizer code under noise $\chan$, each
syndrome $s$ has a data-processing gap $\Delta D(s)$.
Since $\Delta D(s)$ depends only on the noise channel and code
state, the per-distance scaling of rejection rates should be
decoder-independent.  Numerical simulations at
$d \in \{3,5,7,9,11\}$ with three decoders yield a coefficient
of variation $\text{CV} = 0.091$ across decoders, consistent with
this expectation~\cite{SM}.
This is also consistent with the Ising--QEC
mapping~\cite{EnglishEtAl2025}, statistical-mechanical
thresholds~\cite{EnglishWilliamsonBartlett2025}, and scalable
post-selection results~\cite{ChenEtAl2025,SmithBrownBartlett2024}.

\emph{Observation~2: Decoder hierarchy as retrodiction
approximation.}---Since the Petz map is the unique
Bayesian-consistent retrodiction (Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}), all
good decoders must approximate it.  The known decoder
hierarchy~\cite{deMartiOlius2024}---ML $>$ neural
network~\cite{Bausch2024} $>$ MWPM $>$ Union-Find---is consistent
with being ordered by retrodiction fidelity.  A direct test would
require computing the channel divergence
$D(\tilde{\Petz}_{\sigma,\chan}\|\mathcal{D})$ for each decoder
$\mathcal{D}$, which we leave to future work.

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The parameter $\tauasym = 1 - F$ reads the Petz recovery
fidelity as a continuous measure of temporal asymmetry:
$\tauasym = 0$ for closed systems, $\tauasym > 0$ whenever
the process is irreversible.  The equivalence
chain~\eqref{eq:equiv_chain} makes this precise by connecting
$\tauasym = 0$ to vanishing entropy production, the quantum
Markov condition, and detailed balance.
Beyond this interpretive framework, Theorem~\ref{thm:saturation}
gives a complete characterization of when the recovery bound
saturates---the quantum analogue of a sufficient
statistic---and Theorem~\ref{thm:composition} shows that
$\sqrt{\tauasym}$ satisfies a triangle inequality unique to the
standard Petz map.

The decoherence program~\cite{Zurek2003,Zeh1970} provides the
mechanism: environment-induced decoherence produces
entropy ($\Sigma > 0$), forcing $\tauasym > 0$.
The $\tauasym$ framework restates this in the Petz language,
adds the saturation and composition structure
(Theorems~\ref{thm:saturation}--\ref{thm:composition}),
and makes the connection to QEC decoders explicit.

Theorem~\ref{thm:saturation} is proved for $\sigma = I/d$;
the extension to general full-rank $\sigma$ remains open,
as does the continuous-time generalization of
Theorem~\ref{thm:composition}.
For non-Markovian processes, $\Sigma$ can be temporarily
negative~\cite{Breuer2016}, implying transient decreases in
$\tauasym$.  Whether the process tensor
framework~\cite{Pollock2018} extends the equivalence
chain~\eqref{eq:equiv_chain} to this setting is an
important open question, as is a possible holographic
interpretation of $\tauasym$ via the Petz map's role in
entanglement wedge reconstruction~\cite{Chen2020}.

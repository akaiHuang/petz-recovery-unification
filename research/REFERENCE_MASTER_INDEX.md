### Problem 5: Experimental tests of F ≥ exp(−Σ/2)

## Key Findings That Change Our Strategy

1. **Liu-Bai-Scarani 2026 (PRL)**: Retrodiction gives DIFFERENT answers for proper vs improper mixtures. This means decoherence (which converts improper → proper) is irreversible AT THE RETRODICTIVE LEVEL. This is a potential breakthrough for the "AND→OR" problem — τ→1 for improper mixtures IS genuine collapse in the retrodictive sense.

2. **Kuang-Torii-Buscemi 2025**: ALL f-divergences give the same retrodiction for measurement channels. This means τ is not an arbitrary choice — it's THE retrodiction measure for measurements.

3. **Jean et al. 2025**: Time-symmetry is mathematically equivalent to having closed timelike curves. This connects τ=0 (time-symmetric) to CTC structure, potentially supporting the "information time machine" framing.

4. **Galley et al. 2025 (Nature)**: Classical gravity CAN produce entanglement-like signatures, challenging the BMV interpretation. This means the "is gravity quantum?" question remains open, which affects whether τ_grav > 0 is FAPP or genuine.

5. **Bai-Buscemi-Scarani 2025 (PRL)**: Petz map derived from MINIMUM CHANGE principle (variational). This is the strongest foundational justification for τ = 1 − F_Petz as THE measure of irreversibility.

# Reference Index: Quantum Eraser, Retrocausality, and Time-Symmetric QM

**Compiled**: 2026-03-09
**Purpose**: Literature survey supporting the thesis that "the need to wait in quantum erasers is the operational signature of temporal asymmetry" and its connection to the Petz recovery / tau framework.

## I. PRO-RETROCAUSALITY: Core Papers

### 1. Wharton & Argaman (2020) -- Bell's Theorem and Locally-Mediated Reformulations
- **Title**: Colloquium: Bell's theorem and locally mediated reformulations of quantum mechanics
- **Authors**: Ken Wharton, Nathan Argaman
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: Reviews of Modern Physics 92, 021002
- **arXiv**: [1906.04313](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.04313)
- **Summary**: Comprehensive review arguing that Bell's theorem does not exclude all locally mediated models of quantum mechanics. Models where intermediate parameters track particle worldlines can reproduce Bell correlations if one relaxes an arrow-of-time assumption, allowing "future-input dependence" -- mediating parameters that functionally depend on measurement settings in their future. Argues these models are more compatible with special relativity than nonlocal approaches. Introduces the "collider" framework from causal inference to analyze Bell experiments.
- **Relevance to thesis**: Directly supports the idea that temporal asymmetry (the arrow-of-time assumption) is what prevents us from seeing correlations without "waiting." If that assumption is relaxed, locality is restored.

### 2. Leifer & Pusey (2017) -- Time Symmetry Implies Retrocausality
- **Title**: Is a time symmetric interpretation of quantum theory possible without retrocausality?
- **Authors**: Matthew S. Leifer, Matthew F. Pusey
- **Year**: 2017
- **Journal**: Proceedings of the Royal Society A 473, 20160607
- **arXiv**: [1607.07871](https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.07871)
- **Summary**: Proves that if one demands operational time-symmetry in an ontological model of quantum theory (under the assumption of lambda-mediation), then retrocausality is unavoidable. Builds on Huw Price's earlier informal argument but replaces the assumption of quantum state realism with a weaker assumption. Demonstrates that a time-symmetric ontology that fully mediates correlations between preparations and measurements must include backward-in-time causal influences. Subsequently debated by Maudlin (2017).
- **Relevance to thesis**: The formal proof that time-symmetry requires retrocausality directly supports the claim that the "need to wait" (temporal asymmetry) is what prevents us from accessing future measurement outcomes in the present.

### 3. Price & Wharton (2023) -- Why Entanglement?
- **Title**: Why entanglement?
- **Authors**: Huw Price, Ken Wharton
- **Year**: 2023
- **Journal**: Essay (PhilSci Archive / Aeon); arXiv preprint
- **arXiv**: [2212.06986](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.06986)
- **Summary**: Proposes that entanglement arises from collider bias in retrocausal models. In causal inference, a "collider" is a variable influenced by two causes; conditioning on it creates spurious correlations between those causes. In retrocausal quantum mechanics, the EPR source acts as such a collider -- retrocausality from future measurements creates a collider structure at the source, and the resulting "constrained collider bias" explains why entangled particles exhibit correlated outcomes.
- **Relevance to thesis**: If entanglement is a collider artifact of retrocausality, then the quantum eraser's "need to wait" is simply the operational manifestation of needing to condition on the collider (post-select) to reveal pre-existing correlations.

### 4. Adlam (2022) -- Two Roads to Retrocausality
- **Title**: Two Roads to Retrocausality
- **Authors**: Emily Adlam
- **Year**: 2022
- **Journal**: Synthese 200(5), 1-36
- **arXiv**: [2201.12934](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.12934)
- **Summary**: Distinguishes two motivations for retrocausality in QM: (a) using retrocausality to rescue locality (rejecting Bell's theorem conclusions), and (b) accepting nonlocality but insisting on no preferred reference frame, which naturally leads to retrocausality. Argues that motivation (a) is inadequate -- "all-at-once" retrocausality makes locality somewhat redundant. Concludes that the more natural route is (b): nonlocality + Lorentz invariance implies retrocausality, though not via physical systems traveling backwards in time.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The "all-at-once" picture where probabilities are assigned to entire histories resonates with the tau=0 (closed system) scenario where present and future coexist. The need to wait arises only when we break out of this all-at-once picture into an open, temporally asymmetric environment.

### 5. Wharton (2018) -- A New Class of Retrocausal Models
- **Title**: A New Class of Retrocausal Models
- **Authors**: Ken Wharton
- **Year**: 2018
- **Journal**: Entropy 20(6), 410
- **arXiv**: [1805.09731](https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.09731)
- **Summary**: Proposes globally-constrained classical fields as a framework for retrocausal models of quantum phenomena. These models are retrocausal but not retro-signaling, consistent with the block universe viewpoint. Develops example models that resolve problems with classical electromagnetic field descriptions of single-photon phenomena. Shows that average intermediate field intensities connect to quantum "weak values," even in the single-photon limit.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The distinction between "retrocausal" and "retro-signaling" maps onto the tau framework: tau=0 allows perfect retrodiction (retrocausal structure exists) but doesn't enable signaling (no-signaling theorem preserved). The "need to wait" is about signaling, not about underlying causal structure.

### 6. Sutherland (2008, 2017) -- Causally Symmetric Bohm Model
- **Title**: Causally Symmetric Bohm Model
- **Authors**: Roderick I. Sutherland
- **Year**: 2008 (original); updated through 2017
- **Journal**: Studies in History and Philosophy of Science Part B
- **arXiv**: [quant-ph/0601095](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0601095)
- **Summary**: Develops a retrocausal version of Bohm's pilot-wave theory where particle dynamics are influenced by both initial and final wavefunctions. The final wavefunction depends on future measurement settings, creating a "zigzag" causal structure (a la Costa de Beauregard). The model reproduces standard QM predictions, is Lorentz invariant without a preferred frame, and explains Bell correlations via local action-by-contact influences in a 4D spacetime picture.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The zigzag causal structure (forward + backward) is precisely what tau=0 describes: in a closed system, both temporal directions contribute equally. The emergence of tau>0 corresponds to the breaking of this causal symmetry.

### 7. Price (2012) -- Does Time-Symmetry Imply Retrocausality?
- **Title**: Does time-symmetry imply retrocausality? How the quantum world says "Maybe"
- **Authors**: Huw Price
- **Year**: 2012
- **Journal**: Studies in History and Philosophy of Science Part B 43(2), 75-83
- **arXiv**: [1002.0906](https://arxiv.org/abs/1002.0906)
- **Summary**: Argues informally that time-symmetric ontologies of quantum theory should naturally be retrocausal. Retrocausality offers a Lorentz-invariant explanation of Bell correlations without action-at-a-distance. The argument proceeds by noting that if we wouldn't reject forward causation from preparation to measurement, time symmetry demands we equally accept backward causation from measurement to preparation.
- **Relevance to thesis**: Price's argument is the philosophical ancestor of the tau framework's claim: temporal asymmetry (tau>0) is what creates the appearance that we need to "wait" for future measurements. Without that asymmetry (tau=0), both directions are equally accessible.

## II. QUANTUM ERASER: Analysis and Demystification

### 8. Qureshi (2020) -- Demystifying the Delayed-Choice Quantum Eraser
- **Title**: Demystifying the delayed-choice quantum eraser
- **Authors**: Tabish Qureshi
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: European Journal of Physics 41, 055403
- **arXiv**: [1908.03920](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.03920)
- **Summary**: Analyzes the delayed-choice quantum eraser using a Mach-Zehnder interferometer framework instead of the conventional two-slit setup, bringing surprising clarity. Demonstrates that in the delayed mode, there is no which-way information present after the particle is registered -- contrary to popular belief. However, another kind of path information persists. Argues there is no retro-causal effect; rather, post-selection (not causality) is the mechanism by which path information is effectively erased.
- **Relevance to thesis**: Qureshi's analysis that "post-selection, not causality" explains the eraser is entirely compatible with the tau framework. The point is that post-selection requires waiting (temporal asymmetry, tau>0). In a tau=0 system, the post-selection is already determined, and the "erasure" is trivially complete.

### 9. Qureshi (2025) -- The Enigma of Delayed Choice Quantum Eraser
- **Title**: The Enigma of Delayed Choice Quantum Eraser
- **Authors**: Tabish Qureshi
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: arXiv preprint
- **arXiv**: [2510.23539](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23539)
- **Summary**: Follow-up to the 2020 demystification paper, providing further analysis of the quantum eraser puzzle using updated theoretical tools. Continues to argue against retrocausal interpretations by showing that standard quantum mechanics fully accounts for all observed phenomena without invoking backward-in-time influences.
- **Relevance to thesis**: Reinforces that the "mystery" of quantum erasers dissolves under careful analysis -- what remains is the operational question of *why* we must wait (post-select), which is precisely what the tau parameter quantifies.

### 10. Waaijer & van Neerven (2024) -- Delayed Choice in Forward Time
- **Title**: Delayed choice experiments: An analysis in forward time
- **Authors**: M. Waaijer, J. van Neerven
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations
- **arXiv**: [2307.14687](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.14687)
- **Summary**: Provides a strictly mathematical proof that both Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment and the delayed quantum eraser can be fully explained using textbook quantum mechanics, with no information from the future needed. Shows that a modified version with altered time-ordering of experimental steps leads to exactly the same final state, proving the scenarios are completely equivalent regardless of temporal ordering.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The equivalence between delayed and non-delayed versions is precisely what tau=0 predicts: when the system is closed (unitary), time-ordering is irrelevant. The "need to wait" (tau>0) arises only when the system interacts with an open environment that introduces irreversibility.

### 11. Ma, Kofler & Zeilinger (2016) -- Delayed-Choice Gedanken Experiments
- **Title**: Delayed-choice gedanken experiments and their realizations
- **Authors**: Xiao-song Ma, Johannes Kofler, Anton Zeilinger
- **Year**: 2016
- **Journal**: Reviews of Modern Physics 88, 015005
- **arXiv**: [1407.2930](https://arxiv.org/abs/1407.2930)
- **Summary**: Comprehensive review of delayed-choice experiments covering both single-particle (wave-particle duality) and multi-particle (entanglement) scenarios, including quantum erasure and delayed-choice entanglement swapping. Reviews historical development from Wheeler's original thought experiment through modern experimental realizations. Discusses how delayed-choice experiments challenge naive realism and the notion that a quantum system has definite properties independent of measurement.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The most authoritative review of delayed-choice experiments. The key takeaway for the tau framework is that delayed-choice experiments demonstrate that "the past has no definite properties until the future measurement is complete" -- precisely the operational meaning of needing to wait (tau>0) to determine the past.

## III. ANTI-RETROCAUSALITY: Objections and Alternatives

### 12. Wood & Spekkens (2015) -- Fine-Tuning Problem for Retrocausality
- **Title**: The lesson of causal discovery algorithms for quantum correlations: Causal explanations of Bell-inequality violations require fine-tuning
- **Authors**: Christopher J. Wood, Robert W. Spekkens
- **Year**: 2015
- **Journal**: New Journal of Physics 17, 033002
- **arXiv**: [1208.4119](https://arxiv.org/abs/1208.4119)
- **Summary**: Applies causal discovery algorithms to Bell correlations and proves that any causal model explaining EPRB correlations while satisfying no-signaling must violate "faithfulness" -- the assumption that observed statistical independences reflect genuine causal independence. This fine-tuning requirement applies to ALL proposed causal mechanisms: superluminal influences, superdeterminism, AND retrocausal models. Argues this is a serious problem for retrocausal approaches because the no-signaling constraint appears "conspiratorial."
- **Relevance to thesis**: The fine-tuning objection is the strongest argument against retrocausality. However, in the tau framework, the fine-tuning is *explained* by the bound F >= exp(-Sigma/2): the no-signaling constraint is not conspiratorial but is a thermodynamic necessity. The "tuning" is enforced by the second law.

### 13. Hossenfelder & Palmer (2020) -- Rethinking Superdeterminism
- **Title**: Rethinking Superdeterminism
- **Authors**: Sabine Hossenfelder, Tim Palmer
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Physics 8, 139
- **arXiv**: [1912.06462](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.06462)
- **Summary**: Argues that superdeterminism -- the denial of statistical independence between hidden variables and measurement settings -- is a promising approach to solve the measurement problem and understand quantum non-locality. Carefully distinguishes superdeterminism from retrocausality: superdeterminism does not require information traveling backward in time, but rather proposes that initial conditions and measurement choices are correlated via the laws of physics. Proposes "future-input dependence" as less misleading terminology than "retrocausality."
- **Relevance to thesis**: The superdeterminism vs. retrocausality distinction maps onto the tau framework. In a fully deterministic (tau=0) universe, the question of "does the future influence the past?" becomes meaningless -- there is no temporal direction to speak of. Tau>0 is what creates the distinction between "influence from the past" and "influence from the future."

### 14. Carroll (2019) -- The Notorious Delayed-Choice Quantum Eraser (Blog/Informal)
- **Title**: The Notorious Delayed-Choice Quantum Eraser
- **Authors**: Sean Carroll
- **Year**: 2019
- **Source**: Blog post, Preposterous Universe (preposterousuniverse.com)
- **Summary**: Argues from an Everettian (Many-Worlds) perspective that the delayed-choice quantum eraser requires no retrocausality. In the Many-Worlds interpretation, the wave function evolves smoothly with branching rather than collapse, maintaining time-symmetry without backward-propagating signals. The appearance of "erasure" is simply the result of looking at correlations between entangled subsystems; no information is sent backward in time. Only interpretations with wave function collapse might feel pressure to invoke retrocausality.
- **Relevance to thesis**: Carroll's Everettian objection is actually compatible with the tau framework. In MWI, the universal wave function is unitary (tau=0 for the whole universe), and apparent temporal asymmetry (tau>0) arises only for subsystems via decoherence/branching. The "need to wait" is a subsystem phenomenon, not fundamental -- exactly what the tau framework claims.

### 15. Maudlin (2017) -- Time Symmetric QM Without Retrocausality
- **Title**: Time Symmetric Quantum Theory Without Retrocausality
- **Authors**: Tim Maudlin
- **Year**: 2017
- **Journal**: arXiv preprint
- **arXiv**: [1707.08641](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.08641)
- **Summary**: Critiques the Leifer-Pusey (2017) proof that time-symmetry implies retrocausality, claiming the central proof contains a "fatal error." Argues that quantum interpretations can be time-symmetric in both traditional and Leifer-Pusey's stricter sense without requiring retrocausality. Subsequently rebutted by Leifer (arXiv:1708.04364), who argued Maudlin's proposed counterexample does not satisfy the required time-symmetry assumption.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The Maudlin-Leifer debate highlights that the connection between time-symmetry and retrocausality is subtle and depends on precise definitions. The tau framework sidesteps this by providing an *operational* measure (tau = 1 - F) rather than debating ontological questions about the nature of backward causation.

## IV. TIME-SYMMETRIC FORMULATIONS AND RELATED

### 16. Rodriguez-Warnier (2023) -- Temporal Global Correlations
- **Title**: Temporal Global Correlations in Time-Symmetric Collapse Models
- **Authors**: Pascal Rodriguez-Warnier
- **Year**: 2023
- **Journal**: Foundations of Physics 53, 68
- **arXiv**: (available via publisher)
- **Summary**: Proposes a third alternative to the retrocausality debate: time-symmetric quantum mechanics might entail neither retrocausality (contra Price/Leifer-Pusey) nor spooky-action-at-a-temporal-distance (contra Adlam), but rather "temporal global correlations" -- brute correlations between measurement outcomes that are not mediated by any causal mechanism. Examines time-symmetric collapse models by Bedingham and Maroney, which were previously thought impossible for collapse theories.
- **Relevance to thesis**: "Temporal global correlations" without causal mediation is structurally similar to the tau=0 scenario: in a closed system, correlations between past and future exist as brute facts, not because the future "causes" the past or vice versa.

### 17. Jean et al. (2025) -- Equivalence of Time-Symmetry and Cyclic Causality
- **Title**: An equivalence between time-symmetry and cyclic causality in quantum theory
- **Authors**: Eliot Jean et al.
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: arXiv preprint
- **arXiv**: [2508.02463](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.02463)
- **Summary**: Proves a formal equivalence between two extensions of quantum information theory: (a) the multi-time state (MTS) formalism incorporating time symmetry via pre- and post-selection, and (b) the post-selected closed timelike curve (P-CTC) framework enabling cyclic causal influences. Shows that for every multi-time state, there exists an operationally equivalent P-CTC-assisted comb, and vice versa. Establishes a deep mathematical connection between time-symmetry and cyclic causal structure.
- **Relevance to thesis**: This equivalence theorem is remarkable for the tau framework: it says that time-symmetric quantum mechanics (tau=0) is formally equivalent to having closed timelike curves. This supports the interpretation that tau=0 means "present and future coexist" -- the CTC structure makes this literal.

### 18. Dressel, Chantasri, Jordan & Korotkov (2017) -- Arrow of Time for Quantum Measurement
- **Title**: Arrow of Time for Continuous Quantum Measurement
- **Authors**: Justin Dressel, Areeya Chantasri, Andrew N. Jordan, Alexander N. Korotkov
- **Year**: 2017
- **Journal**: Physical Review Letters 119, 220507
- **arXiv**: [1610.03818](https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.03818)
- **Summary**: Investigates the statistical arrow of time for a quantum system under continuous measurement. Shows that time-reversed evolution is always physically possible for non-projective measurements if the measurement record is negated. Despite this dynamical reversibility, a statistical arrow of time emerges, quantified by the log-likelihood ratio between forward and backward propagation hypotheses. Demonstrates that "Janus sequences" of measurements are time-reversed inverses of each other.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The log-likelihood ratio quantifying the measurement arrow of time is directly analogous to tau: both measure how much temporal asymmetry is introduced by the measurement/interaction process. The result that non-projective measurements are always reversible corresponds to tau < 1 (imperfect but nonzero recovery).

### 19. Guryanova, Friis & Huber (2020) -- Ideal Measurements Have Infinite Cost
- **Title**: Ideal Projective Measurements Have Infinite Resource Costs
- **Authors**: Yelena Guryanova, Nicolai Friis, Marcus Huber
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: Quantum 4, 222
- **arXiv**: [1805.11899](https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.11899)
- **Summary**: Proves that performing an ideal projective measurement requires infinite resources (time, energy, or complexity of the measuring device). The third law of thermodynamics imposes this restriction: achieving perfect correlation between system and pointer is infinitely expensive, just as cooling a system to the ground state requires infinite resources. This means all realistic measurements are non-projective and hence partially reversible.
- **Relevance to thesis**: This result has a direct tau interpretation: tau=1 (complete irreversibility / perfect collapse) requires infinite resources. All finite-resource measurements yield tau<1, meaning partial Petz recovery is always possible. The thermodynamic cost of measurement IS the cost of creating temporal asymmetry.

### 21. Gonzalez et al. (2025) -- The Tlalpan Interpretation (QTSI)
- **Title**: Time Symmetry, Retrocausality, and Emergent Collapse: The Quantum Time-Symmetric Interpretation (QTSI)
- **Authors**: (Multiple authors)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: arXiv preprint
- **arXiv**: [2508.19301](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.19301)
- **Summary**: Proposes the "Tlalpan Interpretation" where wavefunction collapse is not a primitive axiom but an emergent phenomenon -- a spontaneous breaking of time symmetry triggered by amplification and record creation. Treats the quantum-classical transition as a phase transition analogous to magnetization in ferromagnets, with measurable order parameters. At the microscopic level, reality is time-reversal invariant (like a block universe); at the macroscopic level, an arrow of time emerges through symmetry breaking. Formulates clear, falsifiable predictions distinguishing it from decoherence or Many-Worlds.
- **Relevance to thesis**: This interpretation is remarkably close to the tau framework's picture: tau=0 (microscopic, time-symmetric) undergoes a "phase transition" to tau>0 (macroscopic, time-asymmetric) through interaction with the environment. The "need to wait" in quantum erasers is a manifestation of this emergent symmetry breaking.

### 22. Stoica (2021) -- The Post-Determined Block Universe
- **Title**: The post-determined block universe
- **Authors**: Ovidiu Cristinel Stoica
- **Year**: 2021
- **Journal**: Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations 8, 69
- **arXiv**: [1903.07078](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.07078)
- **Summary**: Argues that quantum unitary evolution should be taken seriously and that the projection postulate can be understood as unitary and non-discontinuous. The "post-determined" block universe appears to an observer as not being completely determined from the beginning -- each new quantum observation eliminates some possible block universe solutions. The block universe subject to global consistency appears as either "superdeterministic" or "retrocausal" from the perspective of an observer experiencing time flow.
- **Relevance to thesis**: The "post-determined" concept directly corresponds to the tau framework: in the complete block universe (tau=0), everything is determined; for an observer within it (tau>0), the universe appears to be progressively determined as observations are made. The "need to wait" is the observer's experience of progressive determination.

## VIII. KEY FINDINGS FOR THE TAU FRAMEWORK

### Papers that DIRECTLY support "need to wait = temporal asymmetry":
- Leifer & Pusey (2017): Time-symmetry formally implies retrocausality; breaking symmetry (tau>0) creates the need to wait.
- Wharton & Argaman (2020): The arrow-of-time assumption is what prevents locally-mediated models from working.
- Dressel et al. (2017): The measurement arrow of time is quantified by a log-likelihood ratio analogous to tau.
- Guryanova et al. (2020): Perfect measurement (tau=1) costs infinite resources -- temporal asymmetry has thermodynamic cost.
- Jean et al. (2025): Time-symmetry (tau=0) is equivalent to CTC structure -- present and future coexist.

### Papers that are COMPATIBLE but don't explicitly support:
- Qureshi (2020, 2025): Demystifies eraser via post-selection, which is the operational content of tau>0.
- Waaijer & van Neerven (2024): Unitary (tau=0) systems are time-ordering independent.
- Carroll (2019): MWI dissolves the puzzle for the universe (tau=0 globally) but not for subsystems (tau>0).

### Papers that pose CHALLENGES:
- Wood & Spekkens (2015): Fine-tuning problem -- tau framework should show tuning is thermodynamically enforced.
- Maudlin (2017): Disputes Leifer-Pusey proof -- the precise definition of time-symmetry matters.

### Papers that offer ALTERNATIVES:
- Hossenfelder & Palmer (2020): Superdeterminism avoids retrocausality entirely.
- Rodriguez-Warnier (2023): "Temporal global correlations" as a third option beyond retrocausality and action-at-a-distance.
